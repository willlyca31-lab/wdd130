Port-au-Prince

Champ de Mars is the main public square in Port-au-Prince, Haiti, and a central symbol of the nation’s history and civic life. Located in downtown Port-au-Prince, it has long been a place for national celebrations, public gatherings, protests, and cultural events. It is surrounded by important landmarks such as monuments to Haiti’s independence leaders and the National Pantheon Museum (MUPANAH). It reflects both Haiti’s revolutionary heritage and everyday urban life.
